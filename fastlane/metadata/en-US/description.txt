Tray puts the three things you keep reaching for into one edge drawer: clipboard history, a file shelf, and quick notes.

Turn on auto-capture and everything you copy lands in a searchable history with kind icons and pinning. Add a drag-and-drop file shelf for staging files between apps, and a scratchpad that does inline math as you type. Everything is classified and stored entirely on your Mac.

PRIVACY, BUILT IN
Tray keeps your clips, files and notes 100% on-device. Your clipboard history is encrypted at rest with AES-GCM, the key held in the macOS Keychain — the on-disk store is ciphertext, never a readable dump. Items that password managers mark as concealed or transient (1Password and the nspasteboard.org convention) are never recorded. Turn on Private Mode to set a passcode that masks every clip until you unlock — and the passcode itself is stored only as a salted hash, never in plaintext. There is no telemetry in the shipped build and no account. Tray never injects keystrokes into other apps — clicking a clip simply puts it back on the pasteboard, and you press Command-V yourself.

FEATURES
• Auto-capture — copy anything and it appears in history automatically (toggleable, remembered across launches)
• Searchable clipboard history with kind icons (text, URL, color, image)
• Pin the clips you reuse most; delete single clips or clear unpinned
• Copies back verbatim — newlines and indentation preserved
• Private Mode — passcode-masked clips, passcode stored only as a salted hash
• Encrypted history at rest (AES-GCM, Keychain-held key)
• Skips password-manager / transient clipboard items automatically
• Drag-and-drop file shelf with per-type icons — drag files in and back out
• Quick notes with inline calculation (type 12 * 3 and see 36)
• No synthetic paste — sandbox-safe and out of your other apps' input
• 100% local, no analytics, no account

Free and open source.
